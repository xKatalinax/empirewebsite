Gang & Organization Code

Faction Rules

Running a faction on Empire is a privilege. These are the standards every gang, crew, and organization must uphold to stay in the city.

👑Starting a Faction
TicketOpen a Faction Support ticket in Discord to start. You must demonstrate an active roster, solid RP history in the city, and a credible concept before a faction is approved.
RequirementsMinimum active roster, proof of roleplay engagement in the city, and a clear faction identity. Numbers alone do not get factions approved — quality RP and commitment do.
Flag UpYou may not flag up or represent your faction until Faction Support has assigned roles, a current roster screenshot has been submitted, and roles have been granted in the Faction Discord.
AttireFaction members must wear proper gang attire when representing their gang. Clothing must be realistic and recognizable. No faction may use law enforcement or EMS uniforms.
⚔️Conflict & Beef
⚠️ Gang wars must be validated through Faction Support before becoming official. Build the narrative — then bring the smoke.
ValidationConflicts between factions must be escalated through Faction Support before going official. This keeps wars story-driven and fair for both sides.
T-CountMaximum 8 players per side in any criminal conflict. All hostile scenes between two parties carry a minimum 1-hour cooldown after the scene ends.
AccountabilityGang leaders are responsible for their members' conduct. Major rulebreaks by members can affect the entire gang's standing on Empire.
No RandomRandom shooting with no RP context will result in punishment. Build the narrative first — every conflict needs a reason that holds up in an RP context.
💰Drug Spots & Taxing
ClaimingDrug processing locations are claimable on a first-come, first-served basis. Open a ticket with: proof of knowledge of all locations, proof of how to make the drug, possession of $100K worth of the drug, and a clip of taxing someone at a location.
Tax RatesOwning factions may tax civilians up to 25%, other faction members up to 50%, and may charge civilians or factions $50K–$75K weekly to operate at the spot.
FrequencyThe same individual may only be taxed once per hour at a location.
Civ RulesFactions may only rob civilians if: the civilian refuses to pay taxes at a drug spot, initiates violence against the faction, or scams a faction member (rob back scammed items only).
🧍Civilians vs. Factions
Fear RuleCivilians must show fear toward faction members at all times. Civilians may not act violently against factions unless provoked first.
AuthorityFactions cannot target civilians unless the civ refuses to respect their life, refuses to pay taxes, or blatantly disregards faction authority in their controlled zone.
Multi-CharPlayers with faction characters may not run secondary civilian characters to escape faction punishments or act as "super-civs" outside their faction accountability.
📋Faction Roster & Maintenance
UpdatesFaction rosters must be kept current. Submit roster updates in your Faction Support channel. Outdated rosters may result in role removal or faction suspension.
ActivityFactions that go inactive without notifying staff risk losing their faction status, spots, and associated Discord roles. Communicate planned breaks in advance.
LeadershipLeadership changes must be processed through Faction Support. Unauthorized leadership transfers may result in faction suspension.
