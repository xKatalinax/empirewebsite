⚔️ Hostile Interactions 8 RULES
⏱ All hostile interactions must follow minimum time requirements before any violent action.
60 SecStandard areas: Hostile interactions must last at least 60 seconds before shooting or robbing. At least 45 of those seconds must be clearly hostile (threats, arguments, intimidation, not casual talking). There must be valid reasoning for the hostility in the first place.
30 SecIllegal/high-risk areas (drug spots, money wash, black market, harvesting zones): A minimum of 30 seconds of hostile interaction is required before violent action.
2 MinPlayer-owned businesses: Hostile interactions must be a minimum of 2 minutes before violence occurs. You may not rob employees who are on shift or the business owner.
Face-to-FaceHostile actions must begin on foot and face-to-face. The hostile party must start the interaction on foot. If a player drives off after the interaction becomes hostile, you may then pursue and engage from your car. Players may not use vehicles to skip the interaction timer or instantly escalate to shooting.
No PD ShotsYou may never shoot at PD from inside a vehicle under any scenario.
Valid ReasonsValid reasons to escalate to violence include: stealing someone's car, threatening someone's life, or aiming a weapon at someone. Boredom, nice items, or no RP context are never valid.
T-CountMaximum 8 players per side in any criminal conflict. PD is excluded from this limit. All hostile scenes between two parties carry a minimum 1-hour cooldown after the scene ends.
No CampAnti-camping: You may not camp spawn points, hospitals, police stations, job locations, or garages to hunt players. Any stakeout must have active, believable RP, an investigation, surveillance that progresses, or an evolving scene. Passive waiting with no RP engagement is not allowed.

🎭 Roleplay First & Fail RP 6 RULES

If your character is threatened, robbed, kidnapped, injured, or otherwise victimized, you must roleplay through it — these scenarios are an expected part of GTA roleplay.

NVLYour character values their life. In situations where you are outnumbered and/or have a gun pointed at you, you must exhibit genuine fear and comply. A gun must always be feared over a melee weapon.
1v1In a 1v1 situation: you may try to defend yourself only if your weapon is already out. If a gun is already pointed at you, you cannot reach for yours.

3v1+In a 3v1 or greater: if weapons are out, you must fear for your life. You cannot attempt to escape or defend yourself unless your weapon was already drawn.
EqualIn equal-numbers situations, you may attempt to defend or escape, but you accept the risk of dying.

🔁 New Life Rule (NLR) 6 RULES
Memory WipeIf your character is fully downed by another player, they forget everything that happened in the lead-up to their death — events, enemies, conflicts, and storylines tied to that scene.
What Carries OverForgotten: the scene itself — who was involved, why it happened, and any plans or grudges tied to it. Retained: your job, faction ties, and existing relationships and friendships, since those are part of who your character is, not what happened in that scene.
Body CamBody cam footage exists only to report rule violations — it's an OOC tool, not an in-character memory device. Don't reference having body cam footage mid-scene to recall details. The one exception is courtroom roleplay, where it may be referenced as evidence.
No ReturningOnce you leave to get medical attention, that location and its surrounding area are off-limits for that storyline. Don't go back to confront anyone, retaliate, or pick the scene back up.
While DownedOn your side, bleeding out: roleplay your injuries realistically. Flat-backed: /me actions are for describing injuries only — no comms, no planning, nothing scene-related.
TimingDon't use /911ems until the scene has fully concluded, and don't press E to respawn at the hospital during an active scene — wait until it's over.
💪 Powergaming 5 RULES
Forcing ActionsForcing an action ("/me ties you up," "/me shoots you in the head") without giving the other player a reasonable chance to respond or resist is powergaming.
Mechanic AbuseUsing animations, glitches, vehicle exploits, or other mechanics to escape, no-sell damage, or avoid RP consequences entirely is not allowed.
Unrealistic ActionsPerforming actions your character couldn't realistically do given their injuries, equipment, or the situation — fighting at full strength while "unconscious," carrying unrealistic amounts of items — is powergaming.
Combat LoggingCombat logging or disconnecting to avoid the outcome of an RP scenario is powergaming as well as a combat-log offense.
Fair RPRoleplay must remain fair, realistic, and responsive for all parties — both sides need a chance to react. Metagaming alongside powergaming, using OOC info to justify an in-character forced action, is also prohibited.
📡 Metagaming & Streaming Delay 4 RULES
MetagamingMetagaming is strictly prohibited. Your character may only act on information they personally learned through in-game roleplay.
ExamplesThis includes using Twitch/YouTube clips to locate or act on players, reading Discord messages or tickets and applying that knowledge in roleplay, sharing info between characters that wasn't obtained IC, using another player's POV to gain awareness of a scene you weren't part of, relaying scene information to people not present, and being in a Discord call while in the city.
Streaming DelayPlayers who livestream their gameplay (Twitch, Kick, YouTube, TikTok, etc.) must run their stream on a delay of at least 3 minutes to prevent viewers from using live footage to metagame.
Streamer ResponsibilityStreamers are responsible for ensuring their chat or viewers don't relay a player's live location, identity, or situational information into the city. A confirmed instance of a viewer doing so is treated as metagaming, and the streamer may also face action if they knowingly allow it to continue.
🔫 RDM / VDM / Combat Logging 4 RULES
RDMRandom Deathmatch — engaging in violence against another player without proper roleplay interaction or valid in-character justification — is prohibited. All violence on Empire Roleplay must be driven by a clear storyline, motive, or situation.
RP Avoidance ≠ ExcuseA player avoiding or refusing roleplay with you is not valid justification for violence — shooting, robbing, or otherwise harming them in response is still RDM. Continue the interaction as best you can and report the avoidance as Fail RP instead.
VDMVehicle Deathmatch is strictly prohibited. This includes using a vehicle to intentionally harm, kill, or repeatedly strike another player, even in self-defense — vehicles exist for transportation and immersive roleplay, not as weapons.
Combat LoggingCombat logging ("F8 quitting") is any intentional disconnection from the server to avoid roleplay, staff interaction, or in-game consequences — including F8 quitting during an active scene, force-closing the game, unplugging your router, or shutting down your computer to leave.
⚠️ Cheating & Exploiting 4 RULES

🏪 Yellow Zones (Businesses) 6 RULES
ClassificationAll player-owned business locations are classified as Yellow Zones — areas where roleplay is expected to remain consensual and non-hostile. A Yellow Zone covers the interior of the business; if you're unsure of the boundary, ask staff or check the business's designated markers before acting.
Consent RequiredAny interaction initiated inside a business zone (RP scenes, negotiations, sales, confrontations, etc.) requires the clear agreement of everyone involved — an explicit yes, not silence, hesitation, or assumed consent. Staff-organized events or scripted mechanics that override this will be explicitly announced in advance.
Prohibited InsideHostile or violent actions are strictly prohibited inside a Yellow Zone: robbery or theft, kidnapping or forced restraint, shooting or brandishing a weapon threateningly, physical assault, threats of violence to coerce someone into a scene, or forcing anyone into a non-consensual scene. This applies regardless of prior IC history — beef from outside the zone does not carry hostile privileges inside it.
EscalationIf a situation organically escalates toward hostility, all hostile action must stop the moment it would occur inside the zone, and all parties must relocate outside the boundary before any hostile action is permitted. Once clear of the zone, normal hostile RP rules apply — re-entering the zone to escape consequences or reset the situation is not permitted.
Zone MisuseConsidered misuse: baiting/provoking conflict from inside the zone knowing the other party can't retaliate, hiding/escaping mid-scene to dodge death/arrest/robbery, cornering or pressuring a player into an unwanted scene because the zone blocks force, and using the zone as cover or a staging area during a nearby active shootout.
EnforcementViolations should be reported to staff with relevant evidence where possible. Staff will evaluate intent and context — accidental boundary crossings during fast-moving scenes are treated differently than deliberate misuse. Repeated or deliberate abuse may result in warnings, suspension, or further disciplinary action.

⏳ Every hostile interaction — robberies, confrontations, kidnappings — runs on a timer so both sides have a fair, realistic chance to respond.
45 SecStandard interactions: a 45-second timer applies to most hostile interactions — robberies, confrontations, and other scenes that don't take place at a hot spot.
30 SecHot spot interactions (drug locations): a 30-second timer applies, since everyone present already knows the area carries risk. You're allowed to have your weapon already drawn from the start, and the interaction must be played hostile from the very first moment — no friendly bait-and-switch into "hands up." Once a weapon comes out, the scene is locked into the 30-second hostile timer with no walking it back to a peaceful conversation.
JustificationHostile actions — robbing, threatening, attacking, or otherwise targeting another player — must be backed by believable, in-character reasoning. Personal dislike or any other OOC motive is never valid justification. If you can't explain the "why" in character, the interaction shouldn't happen.

Crew SizeMaximum 6 people on scene for a robbery or heist, not including hostages, plus one additional person outside the immediate scene purely as a spotter or outside shooter. Mixing gangs, criminal organizations, and civilians on a crew is only permitted during heists.
Hostage LimitsFleeca Banks and store robberies allow 2 hostages max; all other robberies/heists allow 3 max. Hostages cannot be friends or members of your own gang, and once released or rescued, they cannot rejoin the scene for the rest of that heist.

PD Response WindowOnce PD arrives on scene, the crew has 20 minutes to complete the heist. If the heist isn't completed within that window, PD is cleared to breach and resolve the situation as they see fit.
RestrictionsRefrain from starting any heist within 15 minutes of a restart — doing so will be deemed Fail RP. Helicopters, airplanes, and emergency vehicles are not allowed during heists.
